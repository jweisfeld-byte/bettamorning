# Meta Ad Copy — "Anxious Energy" Angle
Drives to: `anxious-energy-advertorial.html` (publish this page, then use its live URL as the ad's website field)
CTA button: **Learn More**

Format specs used: Primary text (hook front-loaded, ~125 char visible), Headline (≤40 char), Description (≤30 char).

---

## Angle 1 — Pain Point (coffee jitters/anxiety)

**Headlines**
1. Coffee Giving You The Jitters? (30)
2. Your Anxiety Might Be Your Coffee (33)
3. Quit The Coffee Jitters, Not Energy (36)

**Primary Text**
1. I thought my anxiety was just "my personality." Turns out it was my third cup of coffee. Here's what I switched to instead.
2. Coffee gives you jitters. This 200-year-old ritual gives you calm, steady energy — no crash, no anxiety spike. I tried it for 30 days.
3. That tight-chest, can't-sit-still feeling after your 2nd coffee? It might not be "stress." It might be caffeine. Here's the alternative I found.

**Descriptions**
1. No jitters. No crash. (21)
2. Calm energy, no shakes. (23)

**Headlines**

**Descriptions**

2. Steady all day long. (20)

**Primary Text**
1. There's a chemical reason coffee makes some people anxious — and it's not caffeine intolerance. Here's what's actually happening.
2. I found out why coffee made my chest tight and my thoughts race. It wasn't stress. It was the drink.
3. The difference between caffeine and theobromine explains a lot about why coffee and cacao feel so different.

**Descriptions**

## Testing Notes
- Start with 2-3 ad sets, one per angle (Pain Point, Curiosity, and Social Proof are the strongest first bets — Contrarian and Outcome as round 2).
- Rotate 3 primary text variants per ad set against the same creative to isolate what copy angle performs, before testing new imagery.
- Since this funnels to an advertorial (not the product page directly), CTR and article read-through/scroll depth matter as much as clicks — watch for cheap clicks that bounce immediately off the article.
