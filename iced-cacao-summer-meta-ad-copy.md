# Meta Ad Copy — Iced Cacao, "Drink Of The Summer" (Women's Audience)
Drives to: **https://bettamorning.com/icedcacaosummer** (live on the Betta Morning site; use this as the ad's website/destination URL)
CTA button: **Learn More**
Audience: women, sober-curious / mindful-drinking / drinking-less this summer. Voice: first-person woman (matches the advertorial narrator, Maya Ellison) — conversational, aspirational, never preachy.

Format specs: Primary text (hook front-loaded, ~125 char visible), Headline (≤40 char), Description (≤30 char).

Compliance (carry over from the advertorial):
- Never position it as a treatment for alcohol use/dependence or a way to "quit drinking." It's a wind-down / social drink / summer trend.
- Never say "zero caffeine" / "caffeine-free." Cacao has ~15mg caffeine per serving (a fraction of coffee's ~95mg). Differentiate on no-alcohol, no-hangover, no-crash.
- Keep urgency seasonal and honest ("this summer") — no fake countdowns.

## Testing Notes
- Lead with the **Trend** and **Pain Point** angles for a women's summer audience — the "drink of the summer / it-drink" framing matches the advertorial's hook and gives the best ad-to-page congruence. Curiosity and Social as round 2.
- **Creative:** the iced-glass hero (tall frothy cacao) is the scroll-stopper for this angle — it reads as a trendy iced drink women screenshot. Test it against the woman-savoring-her-drink lifestyle shot and the feminine Rose Cacao / tropical-flower styling.
- Rotate 3 primary text variants per ad set against the same creative to isolate the copy angle before testing new imagery.
- Since this funnels to an advertorial (not the product page directly), watch CTR **and** scroll depth / read-through — cheap clicks that bounce off the article are the trap.
- **Audience layering:** sober curious, mindful drinking, "damp lifestyle," non-alcoholic beverages (Athletic Brewing, Ghia, Seedlip, Ritual Zero), functional/adaptogen drinks, wellness, summer entertaining, women 28–55.
- Consider UGC-style video creative of the 60-second iced pour — for a trend/"it drink" angle, showing the drink being made typically outperforms static.
